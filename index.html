Schema Markup Tester

Check if your website's structured data is set up correctly and eligible for Google rich results — star ratings, prices, FAQs, events and more.

What is Schema Markup? Schema markup is a special type of code (structured data) you add to your website that helps Google understand what your content is about. When set up correctly, Google can display your page with rich results in search — things like star ratings, business hours, product prices, and FAQ dropdowns that make your listing stand out and get more clicks.

How to use: Enter your full website URL below, then click the button. Google's tool will open in a new tab and show you exactly what structured data it finds on your page and whether it qualifies for rich results.
🔍 Open Schema Markup Tester on Google
Example: enter https://thewebswan.com → Google opens the tester pre-loaded with your URL
🗺️

XML Sitemap Checker

What is an XML Sitemap? An XML sitemap is a file that lives on your website and lists every important page you want Google to crawl and index. Think of it as a roadmap you hand directly to Google — without it, some of your pages may never show up in search results. Every website should have one, and it should be updated whenever new pages are added.

How to use this tool: Type your domain name below (no need for https://) and click the button. Your browser will open the sitemap file so you can see every URL Google is being told about. If you get a blank page or an error, your site may be missing a sitemap — that's something WebSwan can fix for you!
Example: type thewebswan.com — this opens https://thewebswan.com/sitemap.xml
🤖

Robots.txt Checker

What is robots.txt? The robots.txt file sits at the root of your website (e.g. thewebswan.com/robots.txt) and tells search engine crawlers which pages they are allowed — or not allowed — to visit. This is one of the most overlooked but critical SEO files. A single typo can accidentally tell Google to ignore your entire website, which would wipe it from search results completely.

How to use this tool: Enter your domain name below and click the button. You'll see the raw robots.txt file so you can confirm nothing important is being blocked. After any website launch, redesign, or platform migration — always check this file first!
Example: type thewebswan.com — this opens https://thewebswan.com/robots.txt
🏗️

View Site Structure

What is a Website Structure Analyzer? A website structure analyzer crawls your entire site and maps out how your pages are connected — showing the hierarchy of URLs, internal links, and navigation flow. A well-organized site structure helps Google understand which pages are most important and makes it easier for visitors to find what they need.

Why it matters for SEO: Poor site structure means Google may miss important pages, waste crawl budget on irrelevant ones, and struggle to understand your content hierarchy — all of which hurt rankings. This tool gives you a full visual map so you can spot orphan pages, broken link paths, and structural issues before they become a problem.
🏗️ Open Website Structure Analyzer
Opens websitestructureanalyzer.com in a new tab — enter your domain there to generate a full site map
